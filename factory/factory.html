the long & the short of it

long scroll:the good stuff

visual creativity

There's more visual freedom in parallax website design,Rather than
refreshing and engagging visuals such as animations or beautiful
photography that act as a continuum,ultimately inspiring users to
scroll further down the page.

story-telling opportunities

Do you want to tell a story with your product or service?
long-scrolling lends itself well to a narrative and
encourages meaningful interaction from users.

simple interface for
smart device users

Mobiles and tablets have evoled the way we engage in websites.Its
now less about clicking and more about scrolling,swiping and tapping.
Long-scrolling websites take away the barrier of complex navigavtion,
providing a responsive ,user-friendly experience for mobile users.

punchy staright to
the point messaging

parallax website design is great solution for us
time -poor website browsers,as it forces buisness to convey their
message and vision into one dyanmic web design page.

fashier for users to
realise their goal

these days, scrolling is so simple that it's become an
instinctive action,whereas clicking though to another page
requires thought and motivation.A single page website
allows users to identity their purpose on the website quicker,
than needing to sit through multiples pages.

Long-scroll:the not so good stuff

you need to be search engine savvy

One of the biggest problems that parallax websites face is that
they are not SEO friendly .Why?when you limit the number of pages
on your website ,Google has fewer pages to index and
negatively impacts your standing on the search engine results
page(SERP).luckily there are ways around this.

slower load times

50% of website users bounce off the page if it hasnt loaded in 3-5seconds. So the
more high resolution imagery and animations you include,the longer it itakes to
load page. When writing this article,we tested some popular parallax
websites and found that the ones with complex visuals took up to 30 seconds to
load on a mobile device ,whereas simple and minimalist long scrolls websites
loaded within a few seconds.

Akward navigation

instead of hitting the "back" button, users must scroll back
up the page which can be frustrating for users who want to
refer back to a certain element.An element anchored
to a category or selection.
